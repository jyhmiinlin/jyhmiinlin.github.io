## Physician-scientist

Jyh-Miin's research interests include iterative reconstruction of the PROPELLER (Periodically Rotated Overlapping Parallel Lines with Enhanced Reconstruction) technique and spatio-temporal sparse reconstruction. Jyh-Miin's current project focuses on clinical applications of real-time reconstruction, using high-performance computing and graphic processing units (GPUs). 

Jyh-Miin was awarded a PhD from the University of Cambridge in July 2016. Jyh-Miin obtained an MD from the Department of Medicine, National Taiwan University, in 2005. From 2005-2007, he worked at the magnetic resonance spectroscopy (MRS) lab in the Department of Electrical Engineering, National Taiwan University. He served in the Department of Health, Taiwan, in 2007 - 2008. Then he continued his MRS research in 2008 - 2011. From 2011 - 2012, he was a research assistant in the Department of Radiology, Duke University Medical Center, Durham, USA.
