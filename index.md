## Medical imaging scientist

My research interest is magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) reconstruction, including compressed sensing, 
iterative reconstruction of the PROPELLER (Periodically Rotated Overlapping Parallel Lines with Enhanced Reconstruction) technique and spatio-temporal reconstruction. 
My current project focuses on clinical validation of real-time reconstruction, using high-performance computing and graphic processing units (GPUs). 

I had a PhD from the University of Cambridge. 
Previously, I obtained an MD from the Department of Medicine, National Taiwan University, in 2005. From 2005-2007, I worked at the magnetic resonance spectroscopy (MRS) lab in the Department of Electrical Engineering, National Taiwan University. I served in the Department of Health, Taiwan, in 2007 - 2008. Then I continued his MRS research in 2008 - 2011. From 2011 - 2012, I was a research assistant in the Department of Radiology, Duke University Medical Center, Durham, USA.

Full publications can be found on my <a href="https://orcid.org/0000-0002-2028-2899">ORCID</a> page.
